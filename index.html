About the Length Converter

Convert between meters, feet, inches, miles, kilometers, and more. This tool handles both metric and imperial length units, so whether you're reading a recipe in centimeters or a road sign in miles, you can get an exact figure in seconds.

How the conversion works

Length conversions are based on the meter as the international standard (SI) unit. Every other unit here — feet, inches, miles, yards — is defined as a fixed multiple of the meter, so the math is a straightforward multiplication or division. No rounding tricks, no approximations: this converter uses the internationally defined conversion factors (for example, exactly 1609.344 meters per mile).

Frequently asked questions

How many feet are in a meter?
One meter equals approximately 3.28084 feet. This is the standard conversion factor used in construction, real estate, and international measurement contexts.
What's the difference between a mile and a nautical mile?
A standard (statute) mile is 1,609.344 meters, used for road distances. A nautical mile is 1,852 meters, used in aviation and maritime navigation, based on one minute of latitude on the Earth's surface.
Why do some countries use inches while others use centimeters?
It comes down to historical measurement systems — the US, UK, and a few other countries still use the imperial system for everyday length, while most of the world adopted the metric system as an international standard in the 19th and 20th centuries.
