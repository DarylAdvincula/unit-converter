About the Speed Converter

Convert between miles per hour, kilometers per hour, knots, and meters per second. Useful for travel, aviation, sailing, or just understanding a foreign speedometer or weather report.

How the conversion works

Speed is a ratio of distance over time, so each unit here converts based on its distance component (meters, miles, nautical miles) divided by its time component (seconds, hours). Knots specifically use nautical miles per hour, which is why they don't align neatly with mph or km/h.

Frequently asked questions

How many mph is 100 km/h?
100 km/h is approximately 62.14 mph — a common reference point for European speed limits compared to US ones.
What is a knot in speed terms?
A knot is one nautical mile per hour, equal to about 1.15 mph or 1.852 km/h. It's the standard unit in aviation and maritime navigation.
Why do weather reports sometimes use m/s?
Meters per second is the SI unit for speed and is common in scientific contexts and some countries' weather services, particularly for wind speed.
